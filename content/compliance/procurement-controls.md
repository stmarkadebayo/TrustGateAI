# Procurement Controls

Procurement teams should confirm that invoices, purchase orders, delivery evidence, approval trails, and payment records align before funds are released.

Typical control failures include duplicate invoices, split payments below approval thresholds, over-concentration on a single vendor, missing references, and payments that exceed the related purchase order or contract amount.

Good audit practice requires findings to separate deterministic evidence from interpretation and to recommend specific follow-up checks instead of declaring fraud without proof.
