# United States Invoice Review

When a United States invoice support review is performed, the reviewer should check whether the invoice lines up with the known commercial context: purchase order or contract references, vendor identity, dates, and any supporting authorization or service evidence.

An invoice should not be treated as well-supported if it lacks basic commercial anchors such as a recognizable identifier, a date, an amount, or a vendor identity that matches the rest of the packet.

Where multiple uploaded files show conflicting invoice amounts, conflicting business identities, or missing supporting records, the result should be escalated for manual review rather than approved by automation.
