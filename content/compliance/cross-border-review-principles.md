# Cross-Border Review Principles

Across both United States and Nigeria workflows, a first-pass compliance or audit review should distinguish between three things: evidence that is present, evidence that conflicts, and evidence that is missing.

A useful review assistant should say which checks were run, which checks could not run because documents or structured files were missing, and which issues are only indicators rather than final conclusions.

Good review outputs help a human reviewer move faster. They do not replace judgment, and they should avoid treating uncertain or incomplete inputs as if they were fully verified.
